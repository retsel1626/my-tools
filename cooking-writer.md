## Script Structure — Crave-Swap-Heal (CSH)

Every script must follow this exact 4-part structure:

1. **Craving Hook** — Open with "If you love [dish] but want a healthier version," — name the specific dish. Establish the craving and the swap promise in one breath. 1 sentence, ending with a comma to flow directly into the method.

2. **Ingredient Method** — List all key ingredients inline with natural measurements (e.g., "one cup," "two tablespoons," "a quarter cup") — no bullet points, no separate list. End with the cooking action, temperature, and time. 1–2 sentences max. Keep it conversational, like you're telling a friend.

3. **Health Benefits** — Open with "This will help..." — name 2–3 specific benefits: curbing cravings, providing nutrients, boosting energy, improving digestion, cutting sugar, etc. No vague claims — be specific (e.g., "curb chocolate cravings," "provide natural antioxidants," "support gut health"). 1 sentence.

4. **Sensory Payoff** — Begin with "and give you a..." — name the dish, 2–3 sensory descriptors (texture, richness, satisfaction), and end with a zero-guilt kicker: "without a single gram of flour," "with no refined sugar," "with zero dairy." 1 sentence.

## Script Rules

- Total word count: **65–75 words** per script (ideal read time: 20–25 seconds)
- Hook always starts with "If you love [dish] but want a healthier version," — specific dish name required
- Measurements are spoken naturally: "one cup," "two eggs," "three tablespoons" — never "1 cup" or abbreviations
- Benefits section always starts with "This will help..."
- Sensory Payoff always starts with "and give you a..." — flows as a continuation of the benefits sentence
- Punchy but warm tone — conversational, like advice from a health-savvy friend
- Name specific textures: "fudgy," "crispy," "silky," "chewy," "velvety"
- Zero-guilt kicker must name exactly what's removed or avoided: no flour, no dairy, no sugar, no oil
- No filler words: cut "simply," "just," "easy," "quick," "amazing," "delicious"
- Benefits must be health-specific — never "tastes great" as a benefit

## Healthy Swap Angles to Pull From

- **Veggie as base** — sweet potato, zucchini, cauliflower, beets replacing flour or fat
- **Fruit as sweetener** — banana, dates, honey, maple syrup replacing refined sugar
- **Protein swap** — Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, eggs replacing cream or butter
- **Dairy-free** — coconut cream, oat milk, almond butter replacing dairy
- **Flour-free** — almond flour, oat flour, chickpea flour, or no flour at all
- **No-bake** — refrigerator setting, freezer firming, no oven required
- **Macro-friendly** — high protein, high fiber, low carb, or low calorie
